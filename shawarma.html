CHICKEN SHAWARMA

Description

Chicken Shawarma Chicken shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern dish made by marinating chicken in a mixture of spices, such as cumin, coriander, paprika, a nd garlic, and then grilling it on a spit until it is cooked through and slightly charred. The grilled chicken is then thinly sliced and served in a pita bread or wrap with toppings such as lettuce, tomato, onion, and a sauce, such as tahini or yogurt. Shawarma is a delicious and satisfying meal that is enjoyed by people of all ages, and it is often served as a sandwich or platter with sides such as rice, hummus, and salads. Some variations of shawarma also i nclude a variety of vegetables, such as grilled onions and peppers, for added flavor and nutrition.

Shawarma is a popular street food in many countries in the Middle East, and it is also enjoyed around the world. It is a convenient and tasty meal that can be enjoyed on the go, and it is often served at food trucks or street stalls. Shawarma is typically made with chicken, but it can also be made with other types of meat, such as lamb or beef. The meat is placed on a spit and slowly rotates in front of a heat source, such as a gas or charcoal grill, until it is cooked to perfection. As the meat cooks, the outer layers are shaved off and served in a pita bread or wrap, along with any desired toppings.

Indgredients

  • Chicken
  • Shawarma spice
  • Lemon juice
  • Olive oil:
  • Lemon Juice
  • Pita bread or wraps
  • Toppings(lettuce,pickles, and a sauce such as garlic sauce).
  • Sides(fries,salad,pickles)

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, mix together the shawarma spice blend and lemon juice to create a marinade.
  2. Place the chicken in a large resealable bag or shallow dish and pour the marinade over it. Make sure the chicken is well coated with the marinade.
  3. Marinate the chicken in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or up to 24 hours.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Remove the chicken from the marinade and discard the excess marinade. Brush the chicken with olive oil
  6. Grill the chicken for 4-5 minutes per side, or until it is cooked through and slightly charred
  7. Thinly slice the grilled chicken.
  8. Warm the pita bread or wraps on the grill for a few seconds on each side.
  9. Assemble the shawarma by placing the sliced chicken in the pita bread or wrap and adding your desired toppings.
  10. Serve the shawarma immediately, with any desired sides. Enjoy!
