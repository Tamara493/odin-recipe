TABOULEH

Description

Tabouleh is a refreshing and flavorful Lebanese salad made with bulgur, finely chopped parsley, mint, tomato, and onion. It is typically seasoned with olive oil, lemon juice, and spices such as garlic and ground cumin. The ingredients are mixed together to create a light and aromatic dish that is perfect for a hot summer day.

Tabouleh is often served as a side or appetizer, but it can also be enjoyed on its own as a light meal. It is a popular choice for vegetarians and is often served at potlucks and gatherings. Its bright and colorful appearance makes it a welcome addition to any table. In addition to being delicious, tabouleh is also a healthy choice as it is high in fiber and nutrients. Try serving it with pita bread or alongside grilled meats for a complete meal.

Indgredients

  • Parsley
  • Tomatoes
  • Green onions
  • Fine bulgure
  • Lemon Juice
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Rinse the bulgur in a fine mesh strainer and place it in a medium-sized bow.
  2. Add enough cold water to the bowl to cover the bulgur, and let it soak for about 30 minutes or until the bulgur is tender.
  3. Drain any excess water from the bulgur and set it aside to cool.
  4. In the meantime, finely chop the parsley, tomatoes, and green onions.
  5. Once the bulgur is cool, add the chopped vegetables to the bowl.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice and olive oil. Season with salt to taste.
  7. Pour the lemon juice and olive oil mixture over the bulgur and vegetables, and mix everything together well.
  8. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least an hour to allow the flavors to meld.
  9. Serve the tabouleh chilled or at room temperature, garnished with additional chopped parsley and a drizzle of olive oil if desired. Enjoy!
